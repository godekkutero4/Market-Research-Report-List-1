<p><p>The bone void fillers market comprises various types, each serving unique purposes in orthopedic and dental procedures. Demineralized bone matrix provides osteoconductive properties, facilitating bone regeneration. Calcium sulfate acts as a temporary scaffold that gradually dissolves. Collagen matrix incorporates biological factors, promoting cellular activity. Hydroxyapatite mimics natural bone structure, enhancing integration. Tri-calcium phosphate is used for its biodegradability and ability to release calcium ions. Other fillers may include composites or synthetic alternatives, catering to diverse clinical needs.</p></p>
