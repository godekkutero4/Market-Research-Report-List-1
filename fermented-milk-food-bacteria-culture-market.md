<p><p>The fermented milk food bacteria culture market is primarily divided into mesophilic and thermophilic bacteria types. Mesophilic bacteria thrive at moderate temperatures, typically between 20-45°C, and are commonly used in yogurt and certain cheeses, contributing to flavor and texture. In contrast, thermophilic bacteria operate effectively at higher temperatures, around 45-65°C, and are essential for producing products like mozzarella and some types of yogurt. Each type offers unique fermentation characteristics that enhance the quality and variety of fermented dairy products.</p></p>
