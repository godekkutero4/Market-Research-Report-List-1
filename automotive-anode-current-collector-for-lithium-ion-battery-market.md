<p><p>The automotive anode current collector market for lithium-ion batteries is divided into two main types: positive collectors and negative collectors. Positive collectors, typically made from aluminum, facilitate the movement of electrons towards the positive electrode during discharge, enhancing battery efficiency. In contrast, negative collectors, usually made from copper, assist in electron flow towards the negative electrode, playing a crucial role in the battery's charge and discharge cycles. Both collectors are vital for optimizing battery performance and longevity in electric vehicles.</p></p>
