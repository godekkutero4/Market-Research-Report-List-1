<p><p>The Vitamin D tests market includes various testing methods catering to different needs. RIA (Radioimmunoassay) offers sensitive measurement but involves radiation. ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay) is widely used for its simplicity and cost-effectiveness. CLIA (Chemiluminescent Immunoassay) provides high sensitivity and speed. POCT (Point-of-Care Testing) allows for rapid results in clinical settings. LC-MS/MS (Liquid Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry) delivers precise quantification, often used in research and specialized labs. Each method serves specific applications, balancing sensitivity, speed, and complexity.</p></p>
